Modern anesthesia has become impressively safe and effective. However, we are obliged to provide alist of some of the complications that may occur under anesthesia, A patient's age, the extent of his/her existing medical problems, and the type of surgery and anesthesia one receives all play a part in determining the risk of possible side effects.

The Proposed Anesthesia is

1 Intravenous sedation/deep sedation using Propofol

2 Regional/local anesthesia with/without sedation

3 Spinal or epidural with/without sedation

4 General anesthesia with/without a breathing device placed in mouth/throat

Possible complications:

Uncommon

A. Bruising or swelling from the intravenous catheter
B. Transient soreness of throat, neck or other muscles
C. Transient dizziness or lightheadedness
D. Transient nausea and/or vomiting
E. Feeling tired for one or several days after anesthesia

Rare:

A. Vivid dreams for one or several days after anesthesia
B. A headache from spinal or epidural anesthesia

Extremely Rare

A. Irritation of the cornea of the eye
B. Aspiration (the passage of stomach contents into the trachea)
C. Irregular heart beat
D. Inability to give enough oxygen or place a breathing tube into your trachea
E. Nerve damage that may be temporary or permanent
F. Stroke, brain damage, liver damage, death, heart attack
G. Converting from the planned anesthetic to a different anesthetic.
